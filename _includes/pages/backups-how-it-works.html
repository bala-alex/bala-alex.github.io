A simple three-step backup process.

Codekeeper Backups is designed to provide you with a seamless, automated backup solution for your software source code repositories across all major developer platforms. Here's a detailed look at our process:

Step 1

Sign Up and Connect Your Repositories

1.1 Create Your Codekeeper Backups Account.
To get started, sign up for a Codekeeper Backups account by providing your email address and creating a password. After activating your account, log in to access the user dashboard.

1.2 Link Your Developer Platform Accounts.
Codekeeper Backups integrates with GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket, and Azure DevOps. To connect your repositories, navigate to the "Integrations" tab on your dashboard and select the platform you want to link. Follow the on-screen instructions to authorize Codekeeper Backups to access your repositories.

Step 2

Configure Your Backup Preferences

2.1 Choose Backup Frequency.
By default, Codekeeper Backups performs daily backups for all connected repositories. However, you can customize the backup frequency to better suit your needs. Options include hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly backups.

2.2 Set Custom Schedules.
If you need more control over your backup schedule, you can create custom schedules for specific repositories. This feature is perfect for mission-critical projects that require frequent backups.

2.3 Specify Repositories to Protect.
Codekeeper Backups allows you to back up all your repositories or select specific ones to protect. Use the repository management tool on your dashboard to customize your backup preferences.

Step 3

Monitor and Manage Your Backups

3.1 Access Your Backup Dashboard.
The Codekeeper Backups dashboard provides an overview of your backup status, including the number of repositories being backed up, last backup date, and any issues that may have arisen during the backup process.

3.2 Manage Repository Settings.
Easily manage your repository settings from the dashboard, including adjusting backup frequency, custom schedules, and selecting or deselecting repositories for backup.

3.3 Recover Lost Code.
If you ever need to recover lost code, Codekeeper Backups makes it simple. Browse your backed-up repositories, find the desired commit or version, and restore the code with just a few clicks.
